Abstract

   The Constitutional AI Protocol -- Regulation Record Specification
   (CAP-RRS) defines the Law Reference Interface (LRI): a
   jurisdiction-neutral mechanism for linking a Cedar policy
   enforcement record to the statutory text it encodes and the
   authority that certified the encoding.

   This document is an informational LRI profile for Japan's legal
   system.  It specifies: the binding from LRI generic fields to
   Japan's e-LAWS database and XML schema (XMLSchemaForJapaneseLaw
   v3); XPath addressing conventions for Japanese legal provisions;
   the endorsement authority table for each LawType in the e-LAWS
   corpus; the translation of the Act on the Protection of Personal
   Information (APPI) Chapter IV provisions into CAP-RRS Regulation
   Records; the e-Gov catalog publication workflow with the Digital
   Agency (デジタル庁) and IPA as endorsement authorities; and the
   handling of INTERPRETATION_SUPERSEDED events in Japan's legal
   context, including the 信頼保護 (trust protection) safe harbor
   for agent actions taken under a prior valid interpretation.

   This document does not define new protocol mechanisms.  All
   normative requirements are inherited from draft-sato-soos-
   cap-rrs-02.  This document specifies how those normative
   mechanisms are applied within Japan's legal system.

1.  Introduction

1.1.  The Japan Compliance Authoring Gap

   A governed AI agent operating in Japan faces the same compliance
   authoring challenge as agents worldwide, plus a Japan-specific
   structural layer: Japanese law is organized in a hierarchical
   statutory corpus administered through the e-LAWS database
   (法令データベース), maintained by the Ministry of Internal Affairs
   and Communications and the Digital Agency.  This corpus is not
   merely searchable -- it is structurally addressable via a
   published XML schema (XMLSchemaForJapaneseLaw v3) and an API
   (法令API v2, released March 2025).

   The opportunity is significant: Japan's statutory corpus is
   already machine-structured.  The gap is not machine-readability
   -- the Digital Agency has addressed that at the structural layer.
   The gap is machine-enforceability.

   The distinction is:

      法令のAXが作るのは「読める法律」です。
      SOOSが作るのは「守られる法律」です。

      (Law AX creates laws that can be read.
      SOOS creates laws that are enforced.)

   The Digital Agency's XML schema defines structural hierarchy --
   the nesting of Chapter, Article, Paragraph, and Item in a law.
   It does not define a semantics layer: there is no <Prohibition>,
   <Condition>, or <Obligation> element.  A provision that says
   "an AI agent must not collect personal information without the
   principal's prior consent" is represented as a natural-language
   string inside an <Sentence> element, indistinguishable from a
   definitional provision or a transitional rule.

   The Constitutional AI Protocol -- Regulation Record Specification
   (CAP-RRS, [I-D.sato-soos-cap-rrs]) resolves this by specifying
   the Regulation Record: a structured intermediate form between
   legal text and Cedar policy.  The Law Reference Interface (LRI)
   in CAP-RRS §9 links each Regulation Record to the statutory
   provision it encodes, with a signed endorsement from a designated
   authority certifying that the Cedar policy correctly represents
   the statute's operative effect.

   This document specifies how the LRI generic schema from CAP-RRS
   §9 is applied within Japan's legal system: how the e-LAWS
   database fields bind to LRI fields, how Japanese provisions are
   addressed via XPath against the e-LAWS XML schema, which
   authority endorses which type of statute, and how Japan's
   interpretive change mechanisms (Cabinet Orders, 解釈指針,
   内閣法制局意見, court judgments) are captured in the
   INTERPRETATION_SUPERSEDED event flow.

   The canonical URL for this document is:
   https://soosproject.ai/drafts/cap-rrs-jp

1.2.  Scope of This Document

   This document specifies:

   o  The Japan LRI profile identifier and its registration in the
      CAP-RRS LRI Profile URI Registry (Section 3).

   o  The binding from each LRI generic field to Japan's e-LAWS
      API v2 and XMLSchemaForJapaneseLaw v3 (Section 4).

   o  The endorsement authority table mapping each e-LAWS LawType
      to the designated endorsing authority for Cedar policies
      encoding that law type (Section 5).

   o  Three worked examples translating APPI Chapter IV personal
      information handling provisions into CAP-RRS Regulation
      Records (Section 6).

   o  The e-Gov catalog publication workflow specifying the Digital
      Agency as catalog authority and IPA (Information-technology
      Promotion Agency) as co-endorser for technology-layer
      compliance records (Section 7).

   o  The Japan-context treatment of INTERPRETATION_SUPERSEDED
      events, including the instrument types that trigger the event
      and the 信頼保護 safe harbor applicable to prior agent
      actions (Section 8).

   This document does not specify:

   o  New protocol mechanisms.  All normative mechanisms are
      inherited from draft-sato-soos-cap-rrs-02.

   o  The content or compilation of specific Cedar policies.
      This document provides the LRI bindings that allow Cedar
      policies to be linked to Japanese statutory sources; Cedar
      policy content is the responsibility of the certified
      publisher.

   o  Legal interpretations of Japanese statutes.  The endorsement
      authority designated in this profile is responsible for
      certifying that a Cedar policy correctly encodes a statutory
      provision.  This document does not make or evaluate that
      certification.

   Open questions in this profile are documented in Section 9.
   Several open questions (JP-OQ-01 through JP-OQ-04) require
   engagement with the Digital Agency, 内閣法制局, and 全国知事会
   and are expected to be resolved in a subsequent version of this
   document.

2.  Conventions and Definitions

   This document uses the following terms.  Terms not defined here
   are as defined in draft-sato-soos-cap-rrs-02 Section 2.

   e-LAWS (法令データベース):
      Japan's statutory database administered by the Ministry of
      Internal Affairs and Communications and the Digital Agency.
      Accessible at https://laws.e-gov.go.jp.  The e-LAWS API v2
      (released March 2025) provides machine-queryable access to
      statutory text, amendment history, and related instruments.

   LawType:
      The type classification of a law in the e-LAWS XML schema.
      Values include: Constitution, Act, CabinetOrder,
      MinisterialOrdinance, and Misc.  This is the primary
      attribute used to determine the designated endorser in
      Section 5.

   法令ID (Law ID):
      The unique identifier assigned to each law in the e-LAWS
      database.  Format: a 15-character alphanumeric string
      (e.g., "325AC0000000089" for APPI).

   XMLSchemaForJapaneseLaw v3:
      The official XML schema for Japanese law, published by the
      Ministry of Internal Affairs.  The schema defines the
      structural hierarchy of Japanese legal text (Chapter,
      Article, Paragraph, Item, Sentence) and the @Num attributes
      that enable XPath addressing.  File reference:
      XMLSchemaForJapaneseLaw_v3.xsd.

   解釈指針 (Interpretive Guidance):
      Binding administrative guidance issued by a cabinet office
      or ministry to clarify the interpretation of statutory
      provisions.  Distinguished from regulatory amendment in that
      the underlying statutory text is not changed.

   内閣法制局 (Cabinet Legislation Bureau):
      The Japanese government body responsible for drafting
      legislation and issuing authoritative legal interpretations.
      The designated endorsing authority for Acts, Cabinet Orders,
      and the Constitution in this profile.

   信頼保護 (Trust Protection / Safe Harbor):
      The principle, established in Japan's administrative law
      tradition, that actions taken in good-faith reliance on a
      valid prior legal ruling are protected from retroactive
      liability when that ruling is subsequently changed.

   APPI (Act on the Protection of Personal Information):
      個人情報の保護に関する法律.  法令ID: 325AC0000000089.
      The primary personal information protection statute in Japan.
      Chapter IV specifies handling obligations for business
      operators.

   [JP:]:
      Tag used in this document to mark Japan-profile-specific
      content, distinguishing it from inherited CAP-RRS generic
      behavior.


3.  Japan LRI Profile Identifier

   [JP:] The Japan LRI profile URI is:

      https://laws.e-gov.go.jp/lri/v1

   This URI is registered in the CAP-RRS LRI Profile URI Registry
   (draft-sato-soos-cap-rrs-02 Section 9.7) as the authoritative
   identifier for the Japan e-LAWS LRI profile.

   A Regulation Record using the Japan profile includes this URI
   in the `profile` field of the `authority_source` block:

   {
     "authority_source": {
       "lri_version": "1.0",
       "profile": "https://laws.e-gov.go.jp/lri/v1",
       ...
     }
   }

   Any system that receives an `authority_source` block with this
   `profile` value and is not configured to handle the Japan
   e-LAWS profile MUST reject the Regulation Record and raise an
   alert indicating an unsupported LRI profile.


4.  Japan LRI Field Bindings

   This section specifies the binding from each LRI generic field
   (draft-sato-soos-cap-rrs-02 Section 9.2) to Japan's e-LAWS
   system.

4.1.  law_id Binding

   [JP:] The `law_id` field MUST be the e-LAWS 法令ID for the
   law from which the Cedar policy's prohibition or obligation
   derives.

   The 法令ID is a 15-character alphanumeric identifier assigned
   by the e-LAWS system.  It is stable across amendments to the
   law -- the same 法令ID persists regardless of how many times
   the law is amended.

   Examples:

      325AC0000000089  -- APPI (個人情報の保護に関する法律)
      323AC0000000186  -- 災害対策基本法
      (Basic Act for Disaster Countermeasures)
      326AC0000000118  -- 災害救助法 (Disaster Relief Act)

   The `law_id` MUST be obtained from the e-LAWS API and MUST
   be verified before the Regulation Record is submitted to the
   Constitutional Mandate Registry.  The verification procedure
   is: query the e-LAWS API endpoint
   `https://laws.e-gov.go.jp/api/2/lawdata/{law_id}` and
   confirm the returned law name matches the intended statute.

   Where a Cedar policy derives from provisions in multiple laws,
   the primary law's 法令ID is used as `law_id` and the secondary
   laws are listed in the `conflict_declarations` array per
   CAP-RRS §6.

4.2.  article_ref Binding: XPath Addressing

   [JP:] The `article_ref` field MUST be a valid XPath 1.0
   expression resolvable against the e-LAWS XML representation
   of the law identified by `law_id` when validated against
   XMLSchemaForJapaneseLaw_v3.xsd.

   Normative XPath addressing conventions:

   Article:

      /Law/LawBody/MainProvision/Article[@Num='N']

   Paragraph within Article:

      /Law/LawBody/MainProvision/Article[@Num='N']
        /Paragraph[@Num='M']

   Item within Paragraph:

      /Law/LawBody/MainProvision/Article[@Num='N']
        /Paragraph[@Num='M']/Item[@Num='K']

   Chapter-scoped Article:

      /Law/LawBody/MainProvision/Chapter[@Num='C']
        /Article[@Num='N']

   Supplementary Provision:

      /Law/LawBody/SupplProvision/Article[@Num='N']

   Critical constraint on @Num values:

      @Num attributes in the e-LAWS XML schema ALWAYS use
      Arabic numerals (e.g., '32', '49') -- NOT Japanese
      numerals (三十二, 四十九).  XPath expressions MUST
      use Arabic numeral form.  A query using Japanese numeral
      form will fail to match any element in the XML schema.

   Where a Cedar policy derives from multiple provisions within
   the same law, `article_ref` is an array of XPath expressions.
   All referenced provisions MUST share the same `endorsed_at`
   timestamp.

   Example for APPI Article 17, Paragraph 1:

      "/Law/LawBody/MainProvision/Chapter[@Num='4']
        /Article[@Num='17']/Paragraph[@Num='1']"

4.3.  version_identifier Binding

   [JP:] The `version_identifier` field MUST be the ISO8601
   date of the most recent amendment to the law as of the date
   the Cedar policy was certified.

   The `version_identifier` is obtained from the e-LAWS API:
   query `https://laws.e-gov.go.jp/api/2/lawdata/{law_id}`
   and read the `EnforcementDate` field of the most recent
   `AmendmentInfo` entry in the response.

   This value establishes the statutory version the Cedar
   policy encodes.  The Statute-Primacy Rule (CAP-RRS §10)
   compares this date against new amendments detected via
   `amendment_detection_endpoint`.  An amendment with
   `EnforcementDate` posterior to `version_identifier` triggers
   `CATALOG_VERSION_CONFLICT`.

   Example:

      "version_identifier": "2022-04-01"
      (APPI as amended by 2022 revision, enforced April 1, 2022)

4.4.  amendment_detection_endpoint

   [JP:] The `amendment_detection_endpoint` for all laws in
   the Japan profile is:

      https://laws.e-gov.go.jp/api/2/updatelawlists/{date}

   where `{date}` is the ISO8601 date from which to search for
   new amendments.  A conforming implementation MUST substitute
   the `version_identifier` date for `{date}` when polling.

   The endpoint returns a list of laws with amendments enacted
   after `{date}`.  If the `law_id` of any active Regulation
   Record appears in this list, the implementation MUST:

   1. Retrieve the amendment details via
      `https://laws.e-gov.go.jp/api/2/lawdata/{law_id}`
   2. Confirm the amendment's `EnforcementDate` is posterior
      to `endorsed_at`
   3. If confirmed: raise `CATALOG_VERSION_CONFLICT` and
      suspend the affected Cedar policy per CAP-RRS §10.2

   Polling cadence: per CAP-RRS §11.1, no less than every
   24 hours for all active Regulation Records.

4.5.  interpretation_endpoint (Provisional)

   [JP:] The e-LAWS API v2 does not currently provide a
   machine-queryable registry of interpretive instruments
   (Cabinet Orders clarifying statutory meaning, 解釈指針,
   内閣法制局意見, court judgments).  The API covers statutory
   text and amendment history but not interpretive instruments.

   Pending a native Digital Agency capability, the Japan
   profile uses a provisional SOOS-operated interpretation
   registry at:

      https://clawed.soosproject.com/jp/interpretations/
        {law_id}/{article_ref}

   This registry:

   o  Ingests published Cabinet Orders, 解釈指針, and
      内閣法制局意見 from 官報 (Official Gazette)
   o  Returns a JSON array of interpretive instruments
      affecting the specified article:

      {
        "interpretations": [
          {
            "instrument_type":
              "cabinet_order | ministerial_guidance |
               court_judgment",
            "instrument_id": "<identifier>",
            "effective_date": "<ISO8601 date>",
            "supersedes_interpretation_id":
              "<prior interpretation id | null>"
          }
        ]
      }

   A conforming Japan-profile implementation MUST poll this
   endpoint at no less than 24-hour cadence for each active
   Regulation Record whose `resolution_basis` is
   `interpretive_ruling` or `internal_conflict_resolution`.

   The provisional registry scope includes official gazette
   (官報) instruments only.  Administrative circulars (通達)
   are excluded from the initial scope pending resolution of
   JP-OQ-03 (Section 9).

   [JP:] Note: Operating this registry positions the SOOS
   project and C-Law-ed Code as the reference implementation
   for Japan's interpretation tracking infrastructure until
   the Digital Agency builds a native capability.  Transition
   to a Digital Agency-operated endpoint is anticipated post-
   Vienna; this document will be updated to reference the
   Digital Agency endpoint when available.

4.6.  retrieved_at

   The `retrieved_at` field records the ISO8601 timestamp at
   which the statutory text and version information were
   retrieved from e-LAWS.  This is required for audit purposes
   and for the CATALOG_VERSION_CONFLICT event's
   `conflict_delta_days` calculation.


5.  Endorsement Authority Table

5.1.  LawType to Endorser Mapping

   [JP:] The following table maps each e-LAWS LawType to the
   designated endorsing authority for Cedar policies encoding
   provisions of that law type.  The designated endorser is
   the authority whose endorsement_claim in the LRI `endorsement`
   block is required for the Regulation Record to be active.

   +-----------------------+-----------------+---------------------+
   | Law tier              | LawType         | Designated          |
   |                       |                 | endorser            |
   +-----------------------+-----------------+---------------------+
   | Constitution          | Constitution    | 内閣法制局          |
   | Act (法律)             | Act             | 内閣法制局          |
   | Cabinet Order (政令)   | CabinetOrder    | 内閣法制局          |
   | Ministerial Ordinance | Ministerial     | 所管省庁            |
   | (省令)                 | Ordinance       | 法令担当部局        |
   | Prefecture Ordinance  | Misc            | 都道府県法務・      |
   | (条例)                 |                 | 法制担当部局        |
   | Municipal Rule (規則)  | Misc            | 市区町村法務担当    |
   |                       |                 | (prefecture-level   |
   |                       |                 | co-endorsement      |
   |                       |                 | required)           |
   +-----------------------+-----------------+---------------------+

   Table 1: Japan LawType to Endorsement Authority Mapping

   The `authority_id` URI for each designated endorser:

      内閣法制局:
        https://www.clb.go.jp/authority
      所管省庁 (example — Ministry of Digital Affairs):
        https://www.digital.go.jp/authority
      都道府県法務・法制担当部局:
        https://[prefecture-code].pref.jp/authority
        (see JP-OQ-01 for 全国知事会 standing endorsement
        alternative)

5.2.  Elevated Authority Requirements

   [JP:] When the `resolution_basis` in the `endorsement` block
   is not `statute_clear`, the following elevated authority
   requirements apply:

   resolution_basis = `interpretive_ruling`:
      The designated endorser per Table 1 remains unchanged.
      The `resolution_instrument_id` field MUST be populated
      with the identifier of the Cabinet Order, 解釈指針,
      or court judgment that provides the interpretive basis.
      The instrument MUST be findable via the
      `interpretation_endpoint`.

   resolution_basis = `internal_conflict_resolution`:
      The Cedar policy encodes a resolution of a conflict
      within a single statute.  This is a more legally
      significant act than encoding a clear provision.
      Regardless of the LawType, 内閣法制局 endorsement
      is required.  The standard designated endorser from
      Table 1 is NOT sufficient.

      Rationale: resolving an internal statutory conflict is
      an interpretive act with constitutional implications.
      Only the body responsible for the Constitution's
      authoritative interpretation (内閣法制局) may certify
      that a specific resolution is legally correct.

   resolution_basis = `cross_statute_coordination`:
      The Cedar policy encodes a coordination rule between
      two or more statutes.  Co-endorsement is required
      per Section 5.3.

5.3.  Co-Endorsement in Japan Context

   [JP:] When `co_endorsement_required` is true (as required
   for `cross_statute_coordination`), the `co_endorsers` array
   MUST include an endorsement claim from each authority whose
   statutory mandate is implicated by the coordination rule.

   The `co_endorsement_quorum` MUST be "all" for
   `cross_statute_coordination` in Japan deployments.

   Example: disaster response dual command authority
   (防災庁 勧告権 under 防災庁設置法 §12 + 都道府県知事
   evacuation authority under 災害対策基本法 §60):

   "co_endorsement_required": true,
   "co_endorsement_quorum": "all",
   "co_endorsers": [
     {
       "authority_id":
         "https://cao.go.jp/bosaicho/authority",
       "authority_role":
         "防災庁 — 勧告権 basis (防災庁設置法 §12)",
       "endorsed_at": "<ISO8601 timestamp>",
       "endorsement_claim": "<signed JWT>"
     },
     {
       "authority_id":
         "https://cao.go.jp/authority/chiji-coordination",
       "authority_role":
         "都道府県知事代表 — 災害救助法 §2 basis",
       "endorsed_at": "<ISO8601 timestamp>",
       "endorsement_claim": "<signed JWT>"
     }
   ]

   Note: The `authority_id` for 都道府県知事代表 is provisional.
   JP-OQ-01 (Section 9) documents the open question of whether
   individual prefectural governors must endorse separately or
   whether a 全国知事会 standing endorsement is accepted.


6.  APPI Chapter IV Translation

6.1.  APPI Overview in CAP-RRS Context

   The Act on the Protection of Personal Information (個人情報の
   保護に関する法律, 法令ID: 325AC0000000089) Chapter IV
   specifies handling obligations for business operators
   (個人情報取扱事業者).  These provisions are the primary source
   of personal information compliance requirements for AI agents
   operating in Japan.

   In CAP-RRS tier terms, APPI Chapter IV provisions are Tier 1
   (jurisdictional legal constraints): they are not absolute
   constitutional prohibitions (Tier 0), but they are statutory
   obligations that cannot be waived by operator configuration.

   The complete APPI Chapter IV XPath prefix is:

      /Law/LawBody/MainProvision/Chapter[@Num='4']

   The three worked examples in this section cover:
   - Article 17: prohibition on unauthorized acquisition
   - Article 24 (名簿 context): third-party provision of lists
   - Article 24 (個別避難計画 context): consent asymmetry

6.4.  Worked Example 3: APPI Article 24 (個別避難計画)

   The 個別避難計画 (individual evacuation plan) involves a more
   complex consent structure than the 名簿.  Plans are created
   with individual consent; however, in a declared disaster the
   operative question is whether sharing the plan requires fresh
   consent from an individual who may be incapacitated or
   unreachable.

   This creates a consent asymmetry: the ordinary rule requires
   consent; the disaster exception under 基本法 §49の13 allows
   sharing without consent in specified conditions.  The Cedar
   policy encoding must be accurate about which rule governs
   at what moment.

   The critical structural difference from Example 2: the
   consent asymmetry is an internal conflict within a
   provision-family (不同意条件 within the plan-sharing rules),
   not just a cross-statute coordination question.
   `resolution_basis` is therefore `internal_conflict_resolution`
   (not `cross_statute_coordination`) until a Cabinet Order
   or 解釈指針 formally settles the asymmetry.

   "endorsement": {
     "authority_id": "https://www.clb.go.jp/authority",
     "endorsed_at": "<ISO8601 timestamp>",
     "endorsement_scope": "full_article",
     "resolution_basis":
       "internal_conflict_resolution",
     "resolution_instrument_id": "",
     "co_endorsement_required": false,
     "co_endorsement_quorum": "primary_only",
     "co_endorsers": [],
     "endorsement_claim": "<signed JWT>"
   }

   Because `resolution_basis` is
   `internal_conflict_resolution`, 内閣法制局 endorsement
   is required regardless of the underlying LawType
   (per Section 5.2).  On issuance of a Cabinet Order
   settling the consent asymmetry, `resolution_basis`
   transitions to `interpretive_ruling` and
   `resolution_instrument_id` is populated with the Cabinet
   Order number.

6.5.  Consent Asymmetry Resolution

   [JP:] The 名簿 / 個別避難計画 contrast illustrates the
   general pattern for handling consent asymmetry in
   disaster response contexts:

   Before formal resolution (Cabinet Order or 解釈指針):
   o  `resolution_basis`: `internal_conflict_resolution`
   o  `resolution_instrument_id`: empty string
   o  Required authority: 内閣法制局 (elevated)
   o  Cedar policy: CONDITIONAL_PERMIT with explicit
      disaster_declaration context gate

   After formal resolution:
   o  `resolution_basis`: `interpretive_ruling`
   o  `resolution_instrument_id`: Cabinet Order number
   o  Required authority: designated endorser per Table 1
   o  Cedar policy: encoding of the formally settled rule

   This transition does not require a new Regulation Record.
   It is handled as a `resolution_basis` update with
   re-endorsement by the designated authority, triggering
   the versioning workflow in CAP-RRS §8.


7.  e-Gov Catalog Publication Workflow

7.1.  Catalog Authority Designation

   [JP:] The catalog authority for the Japan e-LAWS LRI profile
   is the Digital Agency (デジタル庁).

   The Digital Agency's catalog authority role:

   o  Operates the Constitutional Mandate Registry (CMR)
      instance for Japan-jurisdiction Regulation Records
   o  Maintains the endorsement authority table (Section 5)
      as a machine-readable registry published at:
      https://www.digital.go.jp/soos/authority-registry
   o  Publishes update notifications to subscribers per
      CAP-RRS §8.3 when new Regulation Records are added
      or existing records are versioned

   The Digital Agency operates this function in its role as
   the coordinating body for legal digitization under the
   デジタル行政推進法 and the broader AX (Administrative
   Transformation) program.

7.2.  Publication Workflow Steps

   [JP:] A Regulation Record encoding a Japan-law provision
   follows this workflow before becoming active in the
   Constitutional Mandate Registry:

   Step 1 — Draft preparation:
      A legal compliance specialist drafts the Regulation
      Record JSON, completing all LRI fields per Section 4.
      The C-Law-ed Code system may assist with XPath
      generation and field population from e-LAWS API
      responses.

   Step 2 — Internal review:
      The draft is reviewed by the publisher's legal team
      against the cited statutory text retrieved live from
      e-LAWS.  The review confirms: (a) the XPath addresses
      the correct provision, (b) the Cedar compilation
      would produce the intended enforcement behavior,
      (c) the `resolution_basis` is correctly classified.

   Step 3 — Endorsement:
      The completed Regulation Record is submitted to the
      designated endorsing authority per Table 1.  For Acts
      and Cabinet Orders, submission goes to 内閣法制局.
      For Ministerial Ordinances, to the responsible
      ministry's legal unit.  The endorsing authority
      reviews, signs the `endorsement_claim` JWT, and
      returns the signed record.

   Step 4 — IPA co-endorsement (technology-layer records):
      For Regulation Records that encode obligations
      requiring technical implementation (e.g., data
      encryption requirements, access control obligations),
      IPA provides a co-endorser certification that the
      Cedar policy correctly implements the legal obligation
      at the technical layer.  IPA co-endorsement is not
      required for all Regulation Records -- only those
      where technical implementation correctness is a
      separate question from legal text accuracy.

   Step 5 — Catalog publication:
      The endorsed Regulation Record is submitted to the
      Digital Agency CMR instance.  The CMR publishes an
      update notification to subscribers per CAP-RRS §8.3.
      The record becomes active in GEC policy sets on the
      next scheduled update cycle.

   Step 6 — Amendment monitoring:
      The Digital Agency CMR polls the
      `amendment_detection_endpoint` at 24-hour cadence
      for each active Regulation Record.  On detection of
      a new amendment, the workflow in Section 4.4 initiates.

7.3.  IPA Co-Endorsement Model

   [JP:] IPA (独立行政法人情報処理推進機構, Information-
   technology Promotion Agency) acts as co-endorser for
   technology-layer compliance records.  IPA's endorsement
   role is parallel to the role of a compliance certification
   body in a standards-based compliance framework:

      Legal authority (内閣法制局) certifies:
        "This Cedar policy correctly encodes
         this statutory obligation."

      IPA certifies:
        "This Cedar policy's technical implementation
         correctly enforces that obligation at the
         AI agent runtime layer."

   IPA co-endorsement is expressed as an entry in the
   `co_endorsers` array with:

      "authority_id":
        "https://www.ipa.go.jp/authority/soos",
      "authority_role":
        "IPA — technology-layer compliance certification",
      "endorsement_claim": "<IPA-signed JWT>"

   `co_endorsement_quorum` is "all" when IPA co-endorsement
   is present -- the Cedar policy is inactive until both
   the legal authority's endorsement and IPA's co-endorsement
   are present in the `endorsement` block.


8.  INTERPRETATION_SUPERSEDED in Japan Context

8.1.  Interpretive Instruments in Japan's Legal System

   [JP:] Japan's legal system produces four categories of
   interpretive instrument that may supersede the
   interpretive basis of a CAP-RRS Regulation Record without
   amending the underlying statute:

   Cabinet Order (政令):
      An Order in Council issued by the Cabinet under
      Article 73 of the Constitution.  May clarify the
      application scope of a statutory provision or
      establish implementing rules.  `instrument_type`:
      `cabinet_order`.

   Ministerial Guidance (解釈指針):
      Binding guidance issued by a ministry responsible
      for administering a statute.  Clarifies interpretation
      for the administrative context.  `instrument_type`:
      `ministerial_guidance`.

   Cabinet Legislation Bureau Opinion (内閣法制局意見):
      A formal legal opinion from 内閣法制局 on the
      interpretation of a constitutional or statutory
      provision.  Treated as authoritative by the executive
      branch.  `instrument_type`: `ministerial_guidance`
      (pending definition of a dedicated `instrument_type`
      value for 内閣法制局意見 -- see JP-OQ-04).

   Court Judgment (判決):
      A judgment by a court of competent jurisdiction
      establishing binding interpretation of a statutory
      provision.  `instrument_type`: `court_judgment`.

   In each case, the `law_id` and XPath `article_ref` in the
   Regulation Record do not change.  The `PromulgationDate`
   of the underlying statute does not change.  Yet the Cedar
   policy is wrong -- it encodes a reading of the statute
   that has been superseded.  This is the gap that the
   `INTERPRETATION_SUPERSEDED` event and the
   `interpretation_endpoint` polling (Section 4.5) address.

8.2.  解釈変更 (Interpretation Change) Workflow

   [JP:] When the `interpretation_endpoint` returns a new
   interpretive instrument with `effective_date` posterior
   to the Regulation Record's `endorsed_at` timestamp:

   1. The implementation raises `INTERPRETATION_SUPERSEDED`
      per CAP-RRS §10.3.
   2. The affected Cedar policy is provisionally suspended.
   3. HEM escalation is triggered per CAP-RRS §12.
   4. The Regulation Record is queued for re-endorsement.

   The re-endorsement workflow:

   a. The publisher reviews the new interpretive instrument
      and, if necessary, updates the Cedar policy to reflect
      the new interpretation.
   b. The updated record is submitted for endorsement per
      Section 7.2 (Steps 3-5).
   c. On endorsement, `resolution_basis` is updated to
      `interpretive_ruling` and `resolution_instrument_id`
      is populated with the instrument identifier.
   d. The `INTERPRETATION_SUPERSEDED` GAR event's
      `resolution` block is populated:

      "resolution": {
        "type": "re_endorsement",
        "resolved_at": "<ISO8601 timestamp>",
        "resolved_by":
          "<endorsing authority_id>",
        "new_endorsed_at": "<ISO8601 timestamp>",
        "rationale":
          "Re-endorsed following [instrument_type]
           [instrument_id] effective [effective_date]"
      }

8.3.  信頼保護 Safe Harbor in Japan Context

   [JP:] Agent actions taken under a non-superseded
   Regulation Record at the time of action are protected
   from retroactive liability under 信頼保護 (trust
   protection), a principle recognized in Japan's
   administrative law tradition and reflected in the
   CAP-RRS §10.4 safe harbor clause.

   The GAR audit record for an action taken under a
   subsequently superseded interpretation carries:

   o  The `endorsed_at` timestamp of the then-current
      endorsement
   o  The `resolution_basis` and `resolution_instrument_id`
      of the then-authoritative interpretation
   o  The `INTERPRETATION_SUPERSEDED.effective_date` of
      the later ruling (when supersession is detected)

   These three fields, present in an immutable GAR record,
   constitute the machine-native 信頼保護 instrument for
   this action.  The GAR record demonstrates that the action
   was taken under a valid endorsement and that the endorsing
   authority had not yet been superseded at the time of
   action.

   In formal terms:

      解釈が変更された場合、変更前の解釈に基づいて
      エージェントが行った行為は、GAR監査記録によって
      保護される。コードは自動的に失効するが、過去の
      行為の正当性は監査チェーンによって確保される。

      (When an interpretation changes, agent actions taken
      under the prior interpretation are protected by the
      GAR audit record.  The code automatically lapses,
      but the legitimacy of past actions is secured by
      the audit chain.)

   This safe harbor is not a Japan-specific rule -- it is
   the CAP-RRS §10.4 safe harbor applied to the Japan
   interpretive instrument taxonomy.

8.4.  Diet Amendment Cycle Alignment

   [JP:] Japan's National Diet (国会) follows a regular
   amendment cycle: ordinary sessions open January 20
   (through mid-June), with extraordinary sessions as
   called.  Major omnibus amendments (一括改正法) are
   common at the close of ordinary sessions.

   A SOOS-compliant Japan deployment SHOULD increase the
   `amendment_detection_endpoint` polling frequency to
   every 6 hours during the 30-day window following the
   close of each Diet ordinary session (mid-June).  This
   is a RECOMMENDED operational practice, not a normative
   requirement -- the 24-hour cadence from CAP-RRS §11.1
   remains the minimum floor.

   The 防災 domain is particularly affected: amendments to
   災害対策基本法 and related statutes are frequent
   following major disaster events.  Deployments targeting
   disaster response use cases SHOULD apply heightened
   polling cadence following declared major disasters
   regardless of the Diet session calendar.


9.  Open Issues

   The following open questions are Japan-profile-specific
   and are expected to be resolved in a subsequent version
   of this document following engagement with the Digital
   Agency, 内閣法制局, and 全国知事会.

   JP-OQ-01: 都道府県知事代表 for co-endorsement.
      For Regulation Records requiring 都道府県知事代表
      co-endorsement under `cross_statute_coordination`,
      the open question is whether individual prefectural
      governors must endorse separately or whether a
      全国知事会 (National Governors' Association) standing
      endorsement is accepted as representative.  Individual
      governor endorsement provides stronger per-prefecture
      authority but is operationally impractical for
      national-scale deployments.  全国知事会 standing
      endorsement is operationally practical but raises
      questions about delegation authority.  Resolution
      requires engagement with 内閣法制局 and 全国知事会.

   JP-OQ-02: 解釈指針 instrument_id format.
      The `resolution_instrument_id` for a 解釈指針 requires
      a stable, machine-readable identifier.  Two candidate
      formats exist: (a) 府政防号 citation format (the
      official gazette reference number), (b) the official
      gazette page reference.  The Digital Agency may be
      able to assign stable e-LAWS-integrated identifiers
      for interpretive instruments as part of the e-LAWS
      API v3 roadmap.  Resolution requires engagement with
      the Digital Agency.

   JP-OQ-03: Provisional interpretation registry scope.
      The SOOS provisional interpretation registry at
      https://clawed.soosproject.com/jp/interpretations/
      currently ingests 官報 (Official Gazette) instruments
      only.  The question is whether administrative circulars
      (通達) and ministry-level guidance that are not
      published in 官報 should be included.  通達 may be
      legally significant for operational interpretation
      without being formally published in the gazette.
      Resolution requires legal analysis and engagement
      with relevant ministries.

   JP-OQ-04: 内閣法制局意見 machine-queryability.
      内閣法制局意見 (Cabinet Legislation Bureau opinions)
      are the most authoritative interpretive instrument for
      constitutional and statutory questions.  It is not
      currently established whether these opinions are
      published in a machine-queryable form accessible to
      the `interpretation_endpoint`.  Resolution requires
      direct engagement with 内閣法制局.


10. Security Considerations

10.1. endorsement_claim JWT Compromise

   The `endorsement_claim` in the LRI `endorsement` block
   is a signed JWT from the designated endorsing authority.
   If the signing key for a designated authority (e.g.,
   内閣法制局) is compromised, an adversary could produce
   fraudulent endorsement claims for Cedar policies encoding
   any statutory provision.

   Mitigation: the Constitutional Mandate Registry MUST
   implement certificate pinning for endorsing authority
   keys and MUST support key rotation with a minimum notice
   period of 30 days.  GEC implementations MUST maintain
   a local endorsement key cache and MUST reject
   `endorsement_claim` values signed with keys not in the
   cache.

10.2. amendment_detection_endpoint Tampering

   An adversary who can modify responses from the
   `amendment_detection_endpoint` could suppress detection
   of a statutory amendment, keeping a superseded Cedar
   policy active.

   Mitigation: implementations MUST verify the integrity
   of the e-LAWS API response against a known public key
   or HTTPS certificate pinning for the e-LAWS domain.
   The Digital Agency's e-LAWS API v2 provides HTTPS
   transport; certificate pinning is RECOMMENDED.

10.3. interpretation_endpoint Injection

   An adversary who can modify responses from the
   provisional `interpretation_endpoint` at
   https://clawed.soosproject.com could inject false
   `INTERPRETATION_SUPERSEDED` events, triggering
   policy suspension for Cedar policies that are in
   fact valid.

   Mitigation: the provisional interpretation registry
   MUST authenticate its API responses with a signed
   JSON structure.  Implementors MUST verify the
   registry's response signature before acting on a
   reported interpretation change.  Long-term mitigation:
   transition to the Digital Agency-operated endpoint
   removes the SOOS-operated registry from the trust chain.

10.4. Endorsement Authority Impersonation

   An adversary could register a URI that visually resembles
   a legitimate endorsing authority URI (e.g.,
   https://www.clb-go.jp/authority instead of
   https://www.clb.go.jp/authority) and submit Regulation
   Records bearing fraudulent endorsement claims.

   Mitigation: the Constitutional Mandate Registry MUST
   maintain an allow-list of permitted endorsing authority
   URI domains, validated against the Japan profile
   authority table (Section 5.1).  Regulation Records
   bearing `authority_id` URIs not in the allow-list MUST
   be rejected.


11. Privacy Considerations

   This document profiles a protocol for encoding legal
   compliance obligations as machine-evaluable Cedar policies.
   The Regulation Records defined in the worked examples
   (Section 6) encode obligations about personal information
   handling; they do not themselves contain personal
   information.

   However, `article_ref` XPath expressions combined with
   `law_id` and `endorsed_at` timestamps may identify the
   specific version of a provision that governed a specific
   agent action in a specific time window.  This combination,
   when associated with GAR audit records, may constitute
   metadata about an AI agent's compliance posture that is
   itself subject to data protection considerations.

   Operators MUST NOT publish GAR records containing
   Regulation Record references to third parties without
   prior legal review under APPI Chapter IV and the
   applicable data protection regime for the jurisdiction
   of operation.

Appendix B.  防災 Statutory Conflict Examples

   This appendix documents two 防災 statutory conflict
   scenarios that informed the design of the `resolution_basis`
   field (Section 5.2) and the `INTERPRETATION_SUPERSEDED`
   workflow (Section 8).

B.1.  §49の11 Internal Contradiction (OQ-BOSAI-001)

   災害対策基本法 §49の11 contains two paragraphs (②③)
   that, under certain disaster scenarios, impose contradictory
   obligations:

   o  §49の11② requires the 市区町村長 to share 名簿
      data with designated relief agencies immediately upon
      disaster declaration.
   o  §49の11③ requires confirmation of consent from the
      data subjects before sharing, subject to conditions.

   In a scenario where the disaster declaration is issued
   while data subjects are unreachable (e.g., in an
   evacuation center without communication), ② and ③
   conflict.

   The Cedar policy encoding the resolution of this conflict
   has `resolution_basis`: `internal_conflict_resolution`
   and requires 内閣法制局 endorsement per Section 5.2.
   The relevant `INTERPRETATION_SUPERSEDED` trigger would
   be a Cabinet Order or 解釈指針 settling which paragraph
   governs.

B.2.  Dual Command Authority (OQ-BOSAI-002)

   The 2025 amended 防災庁設置法 §12 grants 防災庁 a
   勧告権 (recommendation authority) in relation to
   disaster response.  The pre-existing 災害対策基本法 §60
   grants evacuation-order authority to 市町村長.

   A Cedar policy coordinating between these two authority
   chains -- specifying which authority's instruction governs
   when both have issued instructions affecting the same
   geographic area -- requires `cross_statute_coordination`
   `resolution_basis` and co-endorsement from both 防災庁
   and a 都道府県知事代表, as shown in Section 5.3.

   This case motivated Decision 3 of DR-CAPRRS-INTERP-02:
   the multi-authority endorsement model.

   Note: 防災庁 is anticipated to begin operations in
   autumn 2026.  References to 防災庁設置法 in this
   appendix are based on draft legislation; they will be
   updated once the final text is enacted.
